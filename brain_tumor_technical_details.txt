1. THE PREPROCESSING SCIENCE: CLAHE
------------------------------------
MRI scans are not standardized across different hospitals. CLAHE (Contrast Limited 
Adaptive Histogram Equalization) is used to handle inter-patient variability.

- How it works: Unlike standard equalization, CLAHE breaks the image into 8x8 
  tiles and equalizes them individually while "limiting" contrast to prevent 
  noise amplification.
- Clinical Value: It mimics a radiologist's ability to "window" a scan on their 
  screen, ensuring the AI sees the tumor texture regardless of original MRI 
  brightness/contrast settings.

2. THE AUGMENTATION STRATEGY: MIXUP & CUTMIX
---------------------------------------------
Traditional training causes models to "memorize" pixels. Mixup and CutMix use 
"Manifold Regularization" to teach the model complex decision boundaries.

- Mixup: Overlays two images and their labels with transparency.
- CutMix: Cuts a localized patch from one image and pastes it into another.
- The Logic: In medical imaging, the boundary between healthy and malignant 
  tissue is often ambiguous. These techniques force the model to learn 
  "linear" transitions between classes, drastically reducing overfitting.

3. ARCHITECTURE: EFFICIENTNET-V2-S WITH HYBRID POOLING
------------------------------------------------------
The choice of EfficientNet-V2-S provides a State-of-the-Art (SOTA) balance of 
speed and accuracy.

- Hybrid Pooling (GAP + GMP): 
  - Global Average Pooling (GAP) captures the broad structure of the tumor.
  - Global Max Pooling (GMP) focuses on the "highest intensity" pixels.
  - Clinical Benefit: This ensures that small, pinpoint tumors aren't "washed out" 
    by the average surrounding dark pixels, a common flaw in standard AI heads.

4. THE TRAINING ENGINE: ONECYCLELR & ADAMW
-------------------------------------------
Normal training often gets stuck in "local minima" (sub-optimal results).

- OneCycleLR: Accelerates the learning rate to skip over noise, then 
  decelerates it to a near-zero value to settle into the best global minimum.
- AdamW: Decouples weight decay from the gradient update, ensuring consistent 
  regularization (preventing the model from getting "too large" or complex).

5. REGULARIZATION: LABEL SMOOTHING & STOCHASTIC DEPTH
-----------------------------------------------------
- Label Smoothing (0.1): Instead of 100% certainty, we tell the model to remain 
  10% uncertain. This accounts for the natural disagreement found between 
  expert radiologists.
- Dropout (0.4): Randomly deletes 40% of neurons during each training step. 
  This forces the AI to learn "Redundant Knowledge"—detecting the tumor from 
  multiple different visual features rather than just one.

6. EVALUATION METRICS: BEYOND ACCURACY
--------------------------------------
In a medical context, Accuracy is often misleading due to class distribution.

- Macro-F1 Score: The harmonic mean of Precision and Recall.
- Recall: Our priority. It ensures we do not miss a tumor (False Negative).
- Clinical Performance Matrix: Provides a heat-map of where the AI is 
  confident vs. where it is uncertain, supporting trustworthy doctor-AI 
  collaboration.
